The Problem

Multi-turn agents re-pay for their own history

Every turn, most frameworks resend the entire conversation back to the model — the document, the retained tool outputs, the whole transcript. A chart returned on turn 2 is still being paid for on turn 10.

Cost doesn't grow with the work. It grows with the history — and history only ever gets longer.

You pay for turn 1 again on every turn after it.

The Model

From a measured session to an annual budget

We take the measured cost per session and multiply by monthly volume. Nothing more. The three agents that follow are the same unit economics applied to three realistic workloads.

  • Unit cost is measured, not assumed — $0.0180 Colmena vs. $0.1355 competitor average per session.
  • A "session" is one multi-turn, tool-using task comparable to the benchmark.
  • Same model and prompts for everyone — price per token is identical; only the token volume differs.
  • These are a projection from one controlled benchmark — not six separate experiments.

The Honest Trade

Cheaper — without cutting the work

01

Same answers

Identical quality bar. All six systems pass every task in the benchmark — the savings don't come from doing less.

02

Same speed per call

Median per-call latency is at parity — 761 ms for Colmena vs. 738 ms for the fastest competitor. The engine isn't slower to answer.

03

One honest trade-off

Colmena makes a few more round-trips per session (18 vs. 13) — it swaps calls for tokens. If your bill is measured in tokens, that is exactly the trade you want.

Security

Zero secret leakage
